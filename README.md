# Zara-Sales-Analysis
### Dashboard Summary: Zara Seasonal Sales Dashboard

**Objective:**  
This dashboard focuses on analyzing seasonal sales performance for Zara, providing insights into how different seasons impact sales volume and revenue.

**Key Features:**

- **Total Revenue & Season Contribution:** Displays the overall revenue and the percentage contribution of each season to the total sales.
- **Sales Volume Analysis:** Breaks down sales volume by season and product type (e.g., jackets, jeans, sweaters, t-shirts).
- **Material Popularity:** Highlights which materials (like wool, cotton, polyester) are more popular in different seasons.

**Key Insights:**

- **Seasonal Trends:** Certain seasons, like autumn and winter, show higher sales volumes, indicating increased demand during these periods.
- **Material Insights:** Materials like wool and cotton are more favored in colder seasons, while lighter materials, such as linen, are popular in summer.

### For the Second Dashboard:

**Objective:**  
This dashboard extends the analysis by exploring how price segments, promotions, gender-based purchasing behavior, and product positioning impact sales performance.

**Key Features:**

- **Promotion Impact on Price Segments:**
    
    - Analyzes how different price levels (low, medium, high) respond to promotions, showing that lower-priced items tend to benefit more, while higher-priced items may see less favorable effects.
        
- **Gender-Based Sales Comparison:**
    
    - Compares purchasing patterns between women and men, demonstrating that women generate significantly higher revenue, which helps tailor gender-specific marketing strategies.
        
- **Sales Volume by Promotion:**
    
    - Breaks down how sales volume differs between promoted and non-promoted items within each price segment, providing insights into which segments respond best to discounts.
        
- **Product Position Analysis:**
    
    - Examines how the placement of products within the store (e.g., aisle, end-cap, front of store) influences sales. It shows that certain placements, like end-caps, significantly boost sales, offering valuable insights into merchandising strategies.

**Note:** The filters applied in the primary dashboard are also synchronized with this second dashboard, ensuring a consistent and cohesive data analysis experience across both views.
